# Conclusion

AlphaFund argues that recursive self-improvement, stripped of its science-fiction
framing, is a measurable economic process: a corporation recursively improves when
realized gains finance the next cycle of better prediction and deployment, and the
whole loop can be scored by a single standardized statistic, t-RSI. AlphaCOGANT
adds one observation and follows it to its conclusion. The observation is that
AlphaFund's construction is, object for object, an **Active Inference** agent — a
generative model (the EWM), inferred hidden state (the five capital channels), a
filtered observation history (the channel histories), and an Expected-Free-Energy
objective (the marginal-return vector) whose two halves are precisely AlphaFund's
own pragmatic create-rate and epistemic learning-rate.

Following that observation gives a concrete artifact. The firm becomes a generative
model written in **GNN** and produced by the **COGANT** codebase-to-GNN pattern from
an API-complete corporation; inference over the channels respects the firm
filtration by construction; the portfolio optimizer's allocation is Expected Free
Energy minimization with a legible epistemic/pragmatic split; and t-RSI is recovered
as the thresholded EFE-improvement certificate that gates each self-improvement
commit. A small, deterministic, fully-tested engine realizes all of it, and the
manuscript's every number is gated against that engine.

The payoff is not a better forecast — AlphaFund's proprietary surfaces remain
proprietary and out of scope — but a better-conditioned *frame*. Casting recursive
corporate self-improvement as active inference in GNN buys exactly the properties a
self-grading firm most needs and most easily fakes: it cannot peek across time, its
objective is a single auditable scalar, its prose cannot drift from its computation,
and its self-improvement commits leave a reviewable record in one currency.
Recursive self-improvement and active inference turn out to be the same control
problem viewed from two directions; AlphaCOGANT is the dictionary, the model file,
and the engine that let a firm read it in either.
